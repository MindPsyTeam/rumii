The infrastructure gap
You can't deliver proactive care without continuous signal

The policy mandate is clear: intervene earlier, in the community, before patients reach acute services. But current tools only measure what patients tell you, at the moments they're in front of you. Between appointments — where deterioration actually happens — clinical teams are blind.

×
Scheduled reviews and PROMs

Detect change at appointment intervals — weeks or months apart. Behavioural drift that precedes crisis happens between these points.

×
Digital questionnaires and apps

Require active patient participation. Completion rates decline when clinical signal matters most — during low motivation, fatigue, or distress.

×
Waitlist and caseload management tools

Track administrative status, not patient status. A person may deteriorate significantly while waiting, with no mechanism to detect the change.

How Rumii works
Passive sensing. Behavioural intelligence. Proactive insight.

Rumii provides continuous passive behavioural monitoring through smartphone sensors. No patient input required. The phone they already carry becomes the clinical instrument.

Raw sensor signals are transformed through a proprietary phenotyping pipeline. Four behavioural domains — sleep, activity, social contact patterns, and problematic smartphone use — are computed against each patient’s own baseline, not population norms. These domains feed behavioural clusters (such as sleep boundary dysregulation or routine fragmentation) which in turn resolve into recognisable phenotypes: avoidance, agitation, withdrawal, or condition-specific profiles such as an IBD flare signature. A reasoning agent, informed by behavioural science frameworks, evaluates these phenotypic trajectories and surfaces operational intelligence — identifying which patients show signs of behavioural deterioration and when their trajectory warrants attention.

Through Rumii’s own app, patients can also receive adaptive, compassion-focused behavioural support — just-in-time interventions timed to behavioural state, not a fixed schedule.

Privacy by architecture
No content access

Never accesses messages, browsing, app content, or call logs. Captures motion patterns, sleep-wake timing, social contact regularity, and device interaction behaviour.

On-device processing

Sensitive signal processing on-device. Transmitted phenotypes are abstracted and pseudonymised — patterns, not raw data.

NHS IG compatible

Designed for NHS information governance. Patient-level intelligence on pseudonymised identifiers. Cohort analytics aggregated and anonymised.

Population health intelligence
Live insight into how your population is actually doing

Every deployment generates continuous, real-time population-level intelligence — not from self-report surveys or retrospective audit, but from how people actually behave in daily life. This is data that does not currently exist in NHS pathways.

Commissioners and service leads can see whether a pathway is working while it's running — not six months after it ends. Where need is emerging in a particular cohort or geography, the data surfaces it in real time.

Across multiple deployments within a Trust or ICS, the intelligence compounds. Each pathway contributes to a growing evidence base about which behavioural patterns predict deterioration, what intervention timing works, and which patient profiles need earliest attention — across conditions, not just within them. This replaces the traditional commissioning cycle with a continuous feedback loop between service delivery and population outcomes.
