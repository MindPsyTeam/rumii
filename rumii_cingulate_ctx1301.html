The behavioural signal
Patients don't stop taking their medication overnight. They drift.

When non-adherence does occur, we already see it coming. The same pattern repeats across ADHD populations. Patients don't suddenly disengage. Their behaviour shifts weeks before any clinical measure catches it — sleep disrupts, routines fragment, social contact narrows. By the time a refill gap or nurse call flags the problem, the window to intervene has already closed. This is true of the booster-dose switcher in the critical first weeks on CTx-1301, and it's acutely true of the 18–24 year old navigating the transition to adult life.

Subtle behavioural shift
Sleep regularity declines. Social contact begins to contract. Routine adherence softens. Smartphone pick-up frequency starts to fragment — session duration extends, nocturnal use increases. In ADHD populations this pattern is both a symptom amplifier and an early adherence signal.

Drift accelerates
Isolation, routine breakdown, declining medication adherence. Smartphone interaction patterns have shifted markedly — compulsive pick-ups, fragmented short sessions, sustained nocturnal use. For the 18–24 group: identity and stigma drivers amplify the withdrawal. Conversely, improving pick-up patterns at this stage are a real-world indicator that CTx-1301 is working.

What Rumii delivers
Sense. Understand. Intervene. Report.

Rumii is a behavioural intelligence platform that passively monitors patient behaviour in daily life, detects the early signs of disengagement, delivers adaptive just-in-time interventions grounded in behavioural science, and surfaces operational intelligence to PSP teams — without requiring active input from the patient.

1
Passive sensing

Smartphone sensor streams — activity, sleep, sociability, in-person social patterns, routine, and smartphone interaction patterns (pick-up frequency, session duration, nocturnal use) — collected silently via Rumii's app or an SDK embedded in an existing digital surface. Zero patient burden. No questionnaires. No diaries. Smartphone interaction patterns are particularly sensitive in ADHD populations: normalising pick-up behaviour is a measurable treatment response indicator; deteriorating patterns are an early warning signal weeks before clinical measures catch up.

2
Behavioural phenotyping

Raw signals become digital phenotypes: individual-baseline behavioural profiles that reveal disengagement risk, routine disruption, and adherence drift — detected weeks before formal discontinuation. Each patient is measured against their own baseline, not a population norm.

3
Adaptive JITAI

Patients receive just-in-time adaptive interventions — clinically grounded behavioural health support targeting the sleep disruption, routine fragmentation, and social withdrawal that undermine ADHD medication adherence specifically. Timed to the patient's actual behavioural state, not a fixed schedule. Designed for autonomous engagement — no parent or clinician prompt required.

4
Operational intelligence

PSP teams receive phenotypic patterns, behavioural trajectories, and dropout risk scoring — the real-time signal to know which patients need outreach now and which are stable. Aggregated, anonymised cohort analytics provide the evidence base for payer negotiations and programme reporting.
