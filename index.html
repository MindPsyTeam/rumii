The gap
Every existing tool depends on the participant doing something. That's the failure mode.

The tools available today all share the same dependency: they require active participation to function. When a participant is disengaging, active participation is the first thing to stop.

×
ePRO and eCOA

Deliver questionnaires electronically. When a participant disengages, the questionnaire is the first thing they stop completing. The measurement tool goes silent at the moment signal matters most.

×
Patient engagement platforms

Send reminders and motivational content. These require the participant to open the app, read the message, and act. A participant experiencing fatigue, a flare, or low motivation won't do any of these things.

What Rumii is
Behavioural intelligence infrastructure for clinical trials

Rumii provides continuous passive behavioural monitoring through smartphone sensors. No participant input is required. The sensing layer captures behavioural patterns that are clinically meaningful and privacy-preserving: sleep regularity and disruption, in-person social contraction, smartphone use entropy and fragmentation, and night-time phone activity. These signals generate a continuous behavioural stream that Rumii's proprietary phenotyping pipeline transforms into structured intelligence.

This intelligence serves two purposes. Operationally, it identifies which participants are at risk of dropout before they disengage, giving trial teams a window to intervene. Scientifically, it produces complementary behavioural endpoint data that no other instrument in the trial can provide: continuous, objective measurement of behavioural patterns throughout the entire study period.

Rumii deploys as an SDK embedded into the trial's existing digital front end (for decentralised trials), or as its own standalone app (for site-based trials). In both cases, behavioural intelligence is delivered as a data service into the trial team's existing systems. The delivery adapts to the trial. The intelligence doesn't change.

Privacy by architecture

Rumii is designed so that privacy is structural, not policy-dependent. The intelligence it produces doesn't require anyone to see what participants are doing, only how their behavioural patterns are changing.

No content access

Rumii never accesses messages, browsing, app content, or call logs. Sensing captures motion patterns, sleep-wake timing, social contact regularity, and screen on/off states. It detects that behaviour is changing, not what someone is doing.

Phenotypes stay internal

Sensor data is processed into behavioural phenotypes inside Rumii's pipeline. These scores are never shared externally. The trial team receives risk classifications and behavioural transitions on participant ID only.

Analytics are aggregated

All cohort-level analytics and endpoint reporting are aggregated and anonymised. Individual participants appear only as risk tier and trajectory in operational views. No individual behavioural data is surfaced in reporting.

How it works
From passive data to actionable intelligence
1
Passive sensing

The participant's smartphone continuously generates behavioural signals without any active input: sleep-wake patterns, activity regularity, social contact frequency, and screen use fragmentation. Collected via SDK in the trial's own app, or through Rumii's standalone front end.

2
Proprietary phenotyping

Sensor data is transformed through Rumii's pipeline into structured behavioural domains. Phenotypes are computed continuously and held internally. They are the foundation of all downstream intelligence, never shared externally.

3
Reasoning and risk intelligence

Rumii's reasoning agent evaluates phenotype trajectories to generate participant-level risk scores. It identifies early disengagement, classifies likely drivers, and produces actionable alerts and stratifications.

4
Proactive engagement and retention support

For site-based trials using Rumii's own front end, the app includes a proactive behavioural health engagement layer. Adaptive, non-therapeutic support is delivered to participants based on their behavioural state, helping maintain engagement between study visits. For decentralised trials via SDK, risk intelligence is served via API so the trial team can act on the signal through their own channels.

5
Delivered into the trial workflow

Intelligence is served via API, webhook, or Rumii's hosted console, whichever fits the trial's existing systems. The trial team sees who is at risk and when to act. They never see raw data or phenotype scores.

6
Trial and portfolio analytics

Retention analytics and behavioural health endpoint analytics are produced as structured deliverables. At portfolio level, cross-trial intelligence accumulates across every deployment, informing protocol design, retention benchmarks, and scalability evidence.
