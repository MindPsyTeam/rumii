The behavioural signal
Patients don't disengage overnight. They drift.

Across patient support programmes, post-market follow-up, and clinical trials, the same pattern repeats. Patients don't suddenly stop. Their behaviour shifts — sleep disrupts, routines fragment, social contact narrows — weeks before any formal measure catches it. By the time a nurse call or refill gap flags the problem, the window to intervene has closed.

What Rumii delivers
Sense. Understand. Intervene. Report.

Rumii is a behavioural intelligence platform that passively monitors patient behaviour, detects the early signs of disengagement, delivers adaptive just-in-time interventions to support the behavioural health that underpins adherence, and surfaces operational intelligence to PSP teams — all without requiring anything from the patient.

1
Passive sensing

Smartphone sensor streams — activity, sleep, sociability, routine — collected silently. Zero patient burden. No questionnaires, no diaries, no active engagement required.

2
Behavioural phenotyping

Raw signals become digital phenotypes: clusters of behaviour that reveal disengagement risk, routine disruption, and adherence drift — detected weeks before formal disengagement.

3
Adaptive intervention (JITAI)

Through Rumii's own app, patients receive just-in-time adaptive interventions — compassion-focused behavioural support that targets the sleep disruption, routine fragmentation, and social withdrawal that undermine adherence. Timed to the patient's behavioural state, not a fixed schedule. Every interaction generates training signal that sharpens future intervention timing and content.

4
Operational intelligence

At patient level, PSP teams receive phenotypic patterns, behavioural trajectories, and dropout risk intelligence — the real-time signal to know which patients need outreach now and which are stable. Additional analytics packages provide aggregated, anonymised cohort insights into behavioural adherence and functional outcomes — the evidence base for programme reporting and payer negotiations.
