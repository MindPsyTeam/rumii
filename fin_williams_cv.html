The Thesis

Behaviour is biology's missing variable

Genomics, proteomics, metabolomics — the omics revolution maps the machinery of disease. But the single largest predictor of health outcomes isn't in the genome. It's in how we sleep, move, connect, and cope. Behaviour drives 60–80% of treatment outcomes through the psychoneuroimmunological cascade — and until now, no one has modelled it continuously, in vivo, under intervention.

Rumii introduces the Vivome™ — the fifth -ome — a continuous behavioural measurement layer captured through passive smartphone sensing. Four proprietary layers (battery-preserving sensing → behavioural models for sleep, sociability, presence, and habits → digital phenotyping → a clinical reasoning agent generating state-action-response triplets) create a defensible moat that observation-only platforms cannot replicate. The intervention loop generates the causal structure. Without it, you have correlation. With it, you have a world model.

Rumii (Little Beginnings Ltd)

Founder & CEO · 2024 – present · Oxford, UK

Rumii is a deep-tech behavioural health company building the first real-world model of behavioural health under intervention. The core product is a closed-loop sensing-phenotyping-agentic-intervention system — the Vivome — deployed as a B2B SDK and Data-as-a-Service platform for pharma, clinical trials, and health systems.

I conceived the scientific thesis (behaviour as the missing variable in precision medicine via the PNI cascade), designed the clinical architecture end-to-end, and recruited a founding team that includes an Oxford PhD CAIO (former IQVIA), a four-time exited deep-tech CTO, and a CCO with commercial scale-up experience. I am the sole clinical author of the JITAI (Just-In-Time Adaptive Intervention) policy — a three-layer architecture covering trend-based, acute-state, and safety-override interventions grounded in compassion-focused behavioural activation.

Sensing Layer

Battery-preserving passive smartphone sensing capturing sleep, activity, sociability, and environmental presence — signal-layer models Apple cannot replicate without an intervention loop.

Phenotyping Engine

Digital Phenotype Architecture v4: raw signals → derived features → pattern clusters → phenotypes → risk tiering, all relative to rolling 14-day personalised baselines.

Clinical Reasoning Agent

GPT-4.1 (UK South, data-resident) generating state-action-response triplets. Attachment-aware, readiness-modulated, with CFT stage architecture and safety override matrix.

World Model

Every intervention episode persisted as a research-grade observation from day one — building the causal dataset for a simulation API that enables counterfactual reasoning at scale.
